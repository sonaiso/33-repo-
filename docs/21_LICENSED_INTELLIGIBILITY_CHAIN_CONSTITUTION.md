## Scope

This document records the licensed intelligibility chain — the constitutional
sequence by which a `حامل القول` (utterance carrier) becomes a `مفهوم`
(implicit understanding) through ordered, non-substitutable layers.

It binds the layers already covered in prior audit laws — DAL atomic closure,
LAFZI form, PathCard licensed derivation, Independent Ifādah model, and
Hallucination Leap Barriers — into a single ordered chain and forbids any
layer from acting as a substitute for another.

This document is the linking authority for the work scope of subsequent
audit-only laws:

- Dal–Madlul coupling and Mutabaqah/Tadammun/Iltizam (planned `docs/22`);
- Maqam and Qawli relation boundary (planned `docs/23`);
- Mantooq/Mafhoom preservation (planned `docs/24`);
- ʿĀmil/Maʿmūl/Iʿrāb operator constitution (planned `docs/25`);
- GrammarSchoolPolicy audit law (planned `docs/26`).

Each of those documents is governed by the boundaries declared here.

## One Auditable Claim

The licensed intelligibility chain is an ordered, non-substitutable sequence.

```text
No layer substitutes for another layer.
No carrier substitutes for a signifier.
No signifier alone produces the lexicon.
No lexical signified equals the lexical positing (waḍʿ).
No maqam produces truth.
No ifādah suffices for hukm.
No mafhoom exists without a preserved mantooq.
```

Every promotion along the chain requires a declared slot, a license condition,
a blocker check, a visible residual policy, and a trace, exactly as recorded
in `docs/62_HALLUCINATION_LEAP_BARRIER_LAW.md`.

## Acceptance and Rejection Examples

### Carrier does not substitute for signifier

Accepted audit wording:

```text
The utterance carrier is recorded. The signifier-alone analysis remains a
separate audit object with its own slot and residuals.
```

Rejected wording:

```text
The utterance carrier already contains the signifier analysis.
```

LIC-LAW-02 forbids this collapse.

### Signifier alone does not produce the lexicon

Accepted audit wording:

```text
The signifier-alone audit records form and relation. Lexical positing is
deferred to the lexical positing layer with its own license and residuals.
```

Rejected wording:

```text
The signifier alone determines the lexical entry.
```

LIC-LAW-03 forbids this leap.

### Literal signified does not equal the lexical positing

Accepted audit wording:

```text
The literal signified is the immediate form-attached signified. The lexical
positing requires waḍʿ/samaʿ evidence and remains a separate audit object.
```

Rejected wording:

```text
The literal signified already supplies the lexical positing.
```

LIC-LAW-04 forbids this conflation.

### Maqam does not produce truth

Accepted audit wording:

```text
The maqam closes a licensed gap already carried by the prior question or
visible situation. Truth, hukm, and yaqīn remain outside this audit result.
```

Rejected wording:

```text
The maqam creates the missing meaning and therefore the answer is true.
```

LIC-LAW-05 forbids this leap, consistent with `docs/62` HLB-06 and HLB-07.

### Ifādah does not suffice for hukm

Accepted audit wording:

```text
The utterance may complete a speech function in maqam. Hukm and yaqīn remain
outside this audit result.
```

Rejected wording:

```text
The utterance has ifādah, therefore hukm follows.
```

LIC-LAW-06 forbids this promotion, consistent with `docs/61` and `docs/62`
HLB-07 and HLB-08.

### Mafhoom does not exist without preserved mantooq

Accepted audit wording:

```text
The mafhoom candidate is recorded only after the mantooq has been preserved
with its declared ifādah and visible residuals.
```

Rejected wording:

```text
The mafhoom is asserted while the mantooq remains incomplete or contradicted.
```

LIC-LAW-07 forbids this leap.

## Drift Guardrail

Future documentation, schemas, fixtures, or contracts must not flatten the
chain into fewer layers, nor relabel one layer with another's vocabulary.

- `carrier` must not drift into `signifier`;
- `signifier` must not drift into `lexicon`;
- `literal signified` must not drift into `lexical positing`;
- `lexical positing` must not drift into `usage proves positing`;
- `coupling` must not drift into `hukm`;
- `maqam` must not drift into `truth`;
- `qawli relation candidate` must not drift into `licensed qawli relation`;
- `licensed qawli relation` must not drift into `ifādah`;
- `qawl direction` must not drift into `ifādah`;
- `ifādah` must not drift into `hukm`;
- `mantooq` must not drift into `mafhoom`;
- `mafhoom` must not override `mantooq`.
